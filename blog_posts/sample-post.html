Top 5 Tips for Acing Your ABRSM Practical Exam

Preparing for an ABRSM practical exam requires dedication, practice, and smart strategies. Whether you're tackling Grade 1 or aiming for Grade 8, the pressure can be significant. Here at KITA Edu Center, our experienced music instructors have guided countless students through these exams. Here are their top five tips:

1. Master Your Scales and Arpeggios

They might seem tedious, but scales and arpeggios form the technical foundation of your playing. Examiners look for accuracy, fluency, evenness, and appropriate dynamics. Don't just play them; practice them musically. Vary the articulation and dynamics as specified in the syllabus.

2. Know Your Pieces Inside Out

Beyond just playing the notes correctly, understand the structure, style, and emotional content of your chosen pieces. What is the composer trying convey? Knowing the context helps you deliver a more convincing and musical performance. Practice difficult passages slowly and deliberately.

  • Analyze the form (e.g., ABA, Sonata form).
  • Research the composer and the period.
  • Mark key dynamic changes and articulation in your score.

3. Practice Sight-Reading Regularly

Sight-reading often causes anxiety, but regular practice makes a huge difference. Spend 5-10 minutes each practice session sight-reading new material slightly below your current playing level. Look for patterns, key signatures, and time signatures before you start playing.

4. Simulate Exam Conditions

Perform your pieces, scales, and sight-reading for family, friends, or your teacher as if it were the real exam. This helps you manage nerves and identify areas that need more work under pressure. Record yourself playing and listen back critically.

5. Prepare for the Aural Tests

Aural skills are crucial and tested at all grades. Use ABRSM practice apps or work with your teacher on exercises like clapping rhythms, singing back melodies, identifying chords, and recognizing musical features. Consistent practice is key to developing a good ear.

Bonus Tip: On exam day, arrive early, warm up if possible, take deep breaths, and try to enjoy the experience of sharing your music. Good luck from all of us at KITA Edu Center!
